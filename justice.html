Justice

Adala - justice - in 2011 meant accountability: for land seizure, for rigged courts, for a state that could act on people without ever having to answer for it. The NAC was built on desert, but its construction required clearing peri-urban settlements along the eastern fringe - communities farming reclaimed land under arrangements that were never formally titled. When that land was reclassified, most had no legal standing to contest it. The Egyptian government has not answered the following questions.

01

How many people were relocated to make way for NAC construction or land reclassification between 2015 and 2023?

no response
02

What compensation, if any, was paid to residents and farmers whose land was reclassified as state desert under Law 143/1981?

no response
03

Was an environmental or social impact assessment conducted before groundbreaking? If so, has it been published?

not published
04

Which communities were affected by the Phase 1 and Phase 2 clearances, and where were they resettled?

no response
05

What is the total budget allocated to the NAC to date, broken down by funding source?

not audited
06

Under what legal mechanism can residents of the new capital petition their governing authority, given that no municipal elections have been held?

no mechanism
